The Problem

Rules hardcoded in logic are a liability.

In regulated domains — tax, payroll, banking, insurance — rules change constantly. Teams deploy. They pray the old version still runs when an auditor asks to replay a 3-year-old calculation.

Run-IQ treats legal logic as versioned, parameterized data. The engine is stateless infrastructure. Domain knowledge lives in plugins. Every calculation is fully reproducible — down to the exact rule set and engine version that ran it.

By Construction

Six guarantees that can't be disabled.

01 · Determinism
Same input. Same result. Always.

Arithmetic precision is enforced by construction in every calculation model — no floating-point surprises in financial figures. A result from 2021 is mathematically identical when replayed today.

02 · Idempotence
A requestId runs once. Period.

Network retries and worker re-queues never produce double calculations. The engine checks for an existing snapshot before executing anything — by design.

03 · Immutability
Rules cannot mutate in transit.

Rules are strictly read-only throughout the entire pipeline. A checksum is verified at every reception — any tampering is detected immediately and the rule is rejected.

04 · Audit Trail
Why every rule ran — or didn't.

Applied rules, skipped rules, and the reason for each skip are all archived. CONDITION_FALSE, INACTIVE_DATE, INHIBITED_BY_META_RULE — nothing is hidden from the record.

05 · Reproducibility
Replay any calculation, years later.

Snapshots store the full rule objects — not IDs. Engine version, plugin versions, DSL evaluator versions. Everything needed to reproduce the exact result in 2030 is archived today.

06 · Strict Mode
No snapshot. No result.

In strict: true mode, a snapshot save failure throws before the result is returned. A calculation without an archived legal trace simply does not exist.
